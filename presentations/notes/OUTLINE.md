### 1 — Title
"An Overview of Outcomes" · subtitle: *From antimicrobial use to therapeutic outcomes — driving stewardship with data.*
Nora Schrag, Livestock Veterinary Resources. (Nod: building on M. Apley, "Monitoring our way to stewardship?")

### 2 — Why we started: respect for the tool
Motivation: respect for the *power of effective antimicrobials*; act now to keep them working as long as possible. Stewardship = protect efficacy, not merely reduce use.
[Echoes Apley S40: "if stewardship is perceived as not treating a treatable animal, we are probably done."]

### 3 — Picking up where Mike left off (bridge)
Mike showed: monitoring, metrics, and that a single number/target misleads (granularity 2×2; Goodhart's law). The missing dimension = **outcomes.**
[Reference/recreate Apley S36 2×2 + S42 Goodhart quote.]

### 4 — Journey step 1: to measure use, we needed disease context
Regimens as our favored AMU metric — well-defined relationship to reality & disease.
`Regimens = Animal-Year × (Disease Incidence / AY) × (Regimens / Disease event)`.
[Apley S35 decomposition; cite dairy regimens series P7/P8/P6.]

### 5 — Journey step 2: but the real question is — did the drug work?
Therapeutic outcome is the ultimate question. Use measurement was the *means*; outcome is the *end*. Pivot of the whole talk.

### 6 — How we define an outcome (the ICASA method)
Therapeutic Event (TE, regimens grouped ≤7 days) → 30-day outcome window → **Success / Relapse / Sold / Died.**
[ICASA_Disease app screenshot: 7-day TE + 30-day eval controls; cite P13.]

### 7 — We're not alone: this is where the science is going
Human medicine: microbiological cure → composite → **DOOR/RADAR — "moving beyond mortality."** Cattle: FTS/CFR/relapse/chronic; mastitis "What is success?" (BC 27–95%, doesn't predict clinical outcome). The field converged on multi-dimensional outcomes.
[litsearch: Evans 2015; "Moving Beyond Mortality" 2024; mastitis review 2021.]

### 8 — Core insight: outcomes shift each other — a single number lies
Therapeutic success ↓ by **Death** (unambiguously bad) vs ↓ by **Relapse / aggressive retreatment** (ambiguous — investigate). Blue Dairy (mortality-driven) vs Yellow Dairy (relapse + culling driven) — same low number, opposite actions.
[P13 Figure 5 decomposition — rebuild as clean illustrative figure.]

### 9 — The retreatment nuance is real science
Post-Treatment Interval (PTI): retreat too early and "failure" is manufactured (3-day eligibility → higher retreatment). Retreatment drug-switching linked to more resistant isolates. → investigate the *trend*, don't read the number.
[litsearch PTI; bridges to resistance/Bruce.]

### 10 — Why trends over time, within farm context, is the only path to action
Red Dairy metritis 96%→89% success = top quintile → near bottom (small number, big move). Disease incidence 31→6 TE/100CY with success stable = prevention win, not neglect. Context + trend → ask *"why is…?"* not "above/below target."
[P13 examples; explicit answer to Goodhart from S3.]

### 11 — The stewardship payoff (welfare anchor)
Now we can drive appropriate use: **effective → use it; not effective → don't** — anchored in animal welfare. The goals we trend: ↓ mortality, ↓ morbidity, ↑ therapeutic success.
[Ties back to Apley S40 welfare.]

### 12 — The engine: ICASA pipeline + three apps
Data-flow map; three apps as one chain: **Use (FeedlotMetrics) → Outcome (Disease ← we are here) → Value (Econ).** Each gives the others meaning.
[dataflow.html SVG; app screenshots.]

### 13 — Where resistance fits (Bruce bridge)
Levels of measurement: **Use → Resistance (ARGs/wastewater) → Outcome → Welfare.** Validate Bruce's ARG question (real, under-measured frontier). Outcomes are the *decision* layer; resistance measurement stays method-dependent/contested (metaphylaxis: use↑, health↑, resistance not selected — yet other studies differ). Outcomes give resistance data *meaning*, and tell us what to do under uncertainty.
[P12 metaphylaxis; hand to Bruce & Ken.]

### 14 — Close & hand-off
One line: **Trend outcomes over time, within farm context — the only way to drive data-driven, welfare-anchored action.** "Now, Bruce and Ken on how this is measured in poultry."
[Optional: Galsworthy close — outcomes keep us close to the problem.]
