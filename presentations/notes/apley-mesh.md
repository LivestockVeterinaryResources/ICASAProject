# Mike Apley keynote — "Antimicrobial Use Monitoring: Monitoring our way to stewardship?" (ICASA, 4-2-2026)
Source deck in repo root: `Apley ICASA 4-2-2026.pptx` (43 slides; many are figures/charts w/o extractable text). Text dump: apley-deck-text.md.

## The fit in one line
Mike's deck = the **monitoring/metrics/standardization "how did we get here + why single numbers & targets fail"** setup. Nora's = the **"outcomes are the missing dimension that completes the picture and drives action"** payoff. His deck literally ends at the door Nora walks through.

## Suggested division of labor (Mike as prelim/complement)
Mike carries the measurement backstory and the "why a number alone misleads" argument; hands to Nora exactly at the outcome dimension. The four hand-off slides below are the seam.

## Direct mesh points (Mike slide → how it sets up Nora)

- **Slide 1 — "Monitoring our way to stewardship?"** The question mark is the whole meeting. Nora's talk is the proposed answer: outcomes.
- **Slide 11 — FDA coop-agreement accomplishments (dairy/beef):** described data systems, what's missing (young stock, cow/calf/stocker), standardization challenges, and *"Described data by a variety of metrics with comparison of outcomes by different metrics."* → the origin of the multiple-metrics work (P15). Establishes the shared project history Nora builds on.
- **Slide 16 — DCD vs actual recorded regimens** → supports Nora's "regimens tie to reality" point; defined-constant metrics drift from recorded regimens.
- **Slide 23 — "Sales data vs. use data?"** → aligns with the 2:00 PM Randy Singer sales-vs-use session; Mike frames the limitation Nora's regimen/outcome approach answers.
- **Slide 34 — "What is the goal?"** monitoring w/o goal? baseline+reduction? benchmarking? regulatory? Ends with *"Animal welfare is also an important component."* → tees up Nora's welfare anchor directly.
- **★ Slide 35 — Regimens decomposition (KEY HANDOFF):**
  `Regimens = Animal-Year × (Disease Incidence / Animal-Year) × (Regimens / Disease Incidence)`
  with surrounding factors: case definitions, prevention, efficacy, economics, regimen practicality, resistance selection, food safety, regulations. *"The importance of capturing disease data."* *"What changes when disease incidence changes from diagnosed to anticipated?"*
  → This is the algebraic foundation of Nora's "regimens in disease context." Nora picks up the third term (regimens per disease event = RT-ratio) and adds the OUTCOME of those regimens. **Nora's talk = Mike's slide 35 + the outcome axis.**
- **★ Slide 36 — "The need for granularity in benchmarking" (KEY HANDOFF):** 2×2 of **High/Low Use × Presence/Absence of stewardship**:
  - High use + stewardship = judicious use facing a well-characterized disease challenge + prevention.
  - High use + no stewardship = indiscriminate use.
  - Low use + no stewardship = poor disease detection/surveillance — *lack of animal welfare concern.*
  - Low use + stewardship = absence of disease.
  → Mike's 2×2 proves **low use ≠ automatically good, high use ≠ automatically bad.** Nora supplies the measurement that tells the quadrants apart: **disease context + therapeutic outcome.** This is the single tightest mesh in the deck — Nora's outcome decomposition is the instrument that resolves Mike's 2×2.
- **★ Slide 40 — "Hurdles" (KEY HANDOFF + Bruce bridge):** *Is AMR real? Willing to accept risk? Do you contribute?* and the money line: *"If antimicrobial stewardship is perceived as not treating a diseased animal when an antimicrobial would be effective, we are probably done."*
  → This IS Nora's welfare-anchored thesis (use when effective, don't when not). "Is AMR real? / willing to accept risk?" is also the on-ramp to Bruce's ARG/resistance question.
- **★ Slide 42 — Goodhart's law (KEY HANDOFF):** *"When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure."* (Goodhart 1975 / Strathern 1997).
  → Mike gives the theoretical warning against single-number targets; Nora gives the constructive alternative — her P13 line *"elicit questions of 'why is…?' rather than pronouncing… above or below a… target value."* **Perfect call-and-response: Mike states the law, Nora shows how to live within it.**
- **Slides 37–39 — mapping AMU decisions by vets; "at what level are vets prescribing?" (ADR / fever / pathogen / disease / susceptibility); "how many regimens willing to 'spend' to prevent a case?"** → context/decision-support layer; less central to Nora's outcome focus but reinforces "decisions need outcome feedback."
- **Slides 28–33 — data standardization, voluntary vs mandatory reporting, Europe/Netherlands (~30% short of sales)** → the "reporting" pillar of the meeting; mostly Mike's lane, not Nora's, but explains why clean outcome data is hard to get.
- **Slide 43 — closing:** *"Idealism increases in direct proportion to one's distance from the problem."* (Galsworthy) → humility close; Nora can echo (outcomes keep us close to the animal/problem).

## How to pitch it to Mike
Ask Mike to present roughly **slides 1, 11, 23/34, 35, 36, 40, 42** as the prelim. That path lands the audience exactly at "we need disease context and we must not worship a single number/target" — which is Nora's opening premise. Nora then delivers the outcome dimension (Success/Relapse/Sold/Died, in disease+use context, trended within-farm) that completes his slide 35 term and resolves his slide 36 quadrants, and hands to Bruce via his slide 40 "Is AMR real?".
