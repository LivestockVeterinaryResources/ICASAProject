# P13 (CENTERPIECE) — Improving farm-level AMU stewardship benchmarks by reporting AMU in the context of disease pressure AND outcome of therapy
Schrag NFD, Godden SM, Singer R, Lombard JE, Wenz JR, Amrine DE, Lubbers BV, Apley MD.
Front Vet Sci, 2022. DOI 10.3389/fvets.2022.1022557. Open access.
PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9581275/ | Full: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/veterinary-science/articles/10.3389/fvets.2022.1022557/full

## Why it matters to the talk
This paper IS the thesis: report AMU alongside (a) disease pressure and (b) outcome of therapy, trended within-farm over time, to prompt investigation rather than pronounce good/bad. Contains the exact "therapeutic success decomposes into death vs. retreatment" example.

## Metrics & definitions
- **Therapeutic Event (TE):** regimens to an individual cow with gaps never > 7 days; includes AM + non-AM treatments, multiple syndromes.
- **Standardized Treatment Regimen (REG):** individual/consecutive treatment sequence, gaps ≤7 days; drug- and route-specific.
- **TE/100CY** = all therapeutic events ÷ avg yearly cow inventory, per 100 cow-years  → disease pressure proxy.
- **REG/100CY** = antimicrobial regimens ÷ avg yearly cow inventory, per 100 cow-years  → AMU.
- **RT-ratio = REG ÷ TE** → how often antimicrobials are included in therapy for a disease (1.0 = essentially every case gets an AM; low = selective/pathogen-based).
- **Outcome of therapy (30-day window), 4 mutually exclusive states:**
  - **Success:** cow stayed in herd, no further TE within 30 days.
  - **Relapse:** another TE before day 30. (≈ "aggressive/again retreatment")
  - **Sold:** culled before day 30.
  - **Died:** death before day 30.

## THE concrete example (Figure 5 — outcome decomposition)
Therapeutic success is pulled down by DIFFERENT mechanisms that mean different things:
- **Blue Dairy (mastitis):** very low relapse BUT elevated mortality (2016–2018) → failure = death.
- **Yellow Dairy (mastitis):** low success from BOTH high relapse AND high culling. Quote: "the low % success can be attributed to both relapses...and culling decisions."
- Interpretation split: **relapse suggests treatment inadequacy; culling/death may reflect economics or welfare decisions independent of therapy.** → same "low success" number, opposite actions.

## Trends-within-farm matter (small number, big move)
- **Red Dairy metritis:** success 96% (2016) → 89% (2018) looks tiny but = moving from top quintile to second-from-bottom in the farm distribution → warrants investigation.
- **Red Dairy metritis disease incidence:** 31 TE/100CY (2016) → 6 TE/100CY (2019) with success 93%→89% → prevention gains, not diagnostic neglect.
- **Red Dairy mastitis success:** 74% (2016) → 83% (2019); RT-ratio 0.98–1.32 (nearly all mastitis treated).
- **Yellow Dairy mastitis success:** 70/67/63/69% across 2016–2019; RT-ratio 0.29–0.69 (pathogen-based; only 29–69% of cases get an AM).

## Study population
27 US dairy farms, 2016–2019; herd size 211–6,676 (mean 1,195).

## Quotable lines
- "Single parameters are not sufficient for evaluating antimicrobial use." (multifactorial drivers)
- Reports should "elicit questions of 'why is…?' rather than produce statements pronouncing a particular dairy falling above or below a particular (arbitrary) target value."
- Addresses 4 questions: "How much disease is recorded? How much antimicrobial use is recorded? How often are antimicrobials included in therapy for each disease? What is the outcome of therapy?"
- "There is valuable information in determining why two metrics give different pictures of the same systems."
