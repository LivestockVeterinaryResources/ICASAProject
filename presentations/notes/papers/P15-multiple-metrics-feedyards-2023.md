# P15 — The association of multiple metrics for evaluating AMU in U.S. beef feedyards
Apley MD, Schrag NFD, Amrine DE, Lubbers BV, Singer RS. Front Vet Sci, 2023. DOI 10.3389/fvets.2022.1056476. Open access.
PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9848654/

## Why it matters to the talk
Empirical proof that "a single number is uninterpretable": the SAME feedyards rank completely differently depending on which metric you choose. Supports the move from "use metric" toward outcomes, and the "single parameters are not sufficient" thesis.

## Scope
14 US beef feedyards, 2018–2019; 6,460 lots; 936,660 head. Individual animal treatment records + lot-level in-feed records.

## Metrics compared (7 total = numerator × denominator combos)
- Numerators: Regimens (Reg), milligrams (mg), Calendar Days of Administration (CDoA), number of Defined Daily Doses (nDDD), number of Defined Course Doses (nDCD).
- Denominators: head-days (animal years), head-in, kg live weight sold.

## KILLER concrete findings
- **4 of the 14 feedyards appeared in BOTH the lowest-4 AND highest-4 ranked feedyards depending on which metric was used (2018).** → metric choice flips your conclusion.
- nDDD differed from CDoA by >25% in **49.2% of lots**; at feedyard level (2018) 50% differed >25%.
- nDCD differed from Reg by >25% in **46.4% of lots**.
- Numerator correlations 0.68–0.91; denominators uniformly ≥0.90 → **numerator choice, not denominator, drives divergence.**

## Quotable lines
- "The choice of metric matters in the ranking of individual feedyards, especially within the more tightly grouped feedyards."
- "Selection of a numerator has more effect on apparent antimicrobial use than does any one of these tested denominators."
- Adds to literature on "substantial effects of metric choice on conclusions drawn from comparing antimicrobial use across multiple production sites."
