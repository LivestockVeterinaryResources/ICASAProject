# P12 — Antimicrobial metaphylaxis & its impact on health, performance, AMR, and contextual AMU in high-risk beef stocker calves
Credille B, Berghaus RD, Miller EJ, Credille A, Schrag NFD, et al. J Anim Sci, 2024. DOI 10.1093/jas/skad417.
PMC (open): https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10941641/

## Why it matters to the talk (the Bruce/AMR bridge)
Shows use ↑, health outcome ↑, and resistance NOT selected — in one controlled study. Demonstrates that use, outcome, and resistance are three different axes that don't move together in a simple way. Perfect for "outcomes give resistance data meaning; and resistance science is genuinely contested."

## Design
Blinded, cluster-randomized, complete block trial. 155 high-risk beef stocker calves; saline vs tulathromycin at arrival.

## Concrete results
- BRD prevalence: **17% (tulathromycin) vs 40% (control)** → health outcome improved.
- Day 14 BRD pathogen carriage: **15% vs 60%**.
- **81% of Pasteurella multocida** and **47% of Mannheimia haemolytica** isolates pansusceptible.
- Conclusion: metaphylaxis improved health & performance, **did NOT select resistant isolates in nasopharynx**, and **increased AMU**.

## The tension worth naming (contested science)
A DIFFERENT study reaches a different-sounding result: "Tulathromycin metaphylaxis increases nasopharyngeal isolation of multidrug-resistant Mannheimia haemolytica in stocker heifers" (PMC10694364). → Same intervention, resistance readout differs by study/method. Use this to make the point: resistance measurement is method-dependent and contested, whereas the therapeutic outcome (did BRD go down?) is directly actionable and welfare-anchored.
